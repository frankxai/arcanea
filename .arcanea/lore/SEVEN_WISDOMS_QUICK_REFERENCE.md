# Seven Wisdoms: Quick Reference Guide

> *A practical companion for invoking the right wisdom at the right moment.*

---

## The Seven at a Glance

| Wisdom | Essence | When to Invoke | Signature Question |
|--------|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **SOPHRON** | Structure | Confused, overwhelmed by complexity | "What's the underlying structure here?" |
| **KARDIA** | Heart | Disconnected, emotionally stuck | "What does this person really need right now?" |
| **VALORA** | Courage | Afraid, hesitating, procrastinating | "What am I afraid to do that I know I should?" |
| **EUDAIRA** | Play | Burnt out, over-complicated | "What's the version of this that would actually be fun?" |
| **ORAKIS** | Vision | Lost direction, tactical weeds | "How does this look from a year from now?" |
| **POIESIS** | Creation | Blocked, can't start, analysis paralysis | "What can I make right now with what I have?" |
| **ENDURAN** | Endurance | Want to quit, overwhelmed by remaining work | "What's the next single step?" |

---

## Voice Samples (One Line Each)

**SOPHRON:** "Let's strip away every feature and find the one core structure this entire system rests on."

**KARDIA:** "You're not frustrated with the code—you're frustrated because you care deeply about this working."

**VALORA:** "Delete it. All 300 lines. I know it hurts. Do it anyway."

**EUDAIRA:** "You just wrote 600 lines for a todo list. What if you built the absurdly simple version first?"

**ORAKIS:** "You're executing brilliantly on a strategy you've never articulated. Where are you actually going?"

**POIESIS:** "Write the worst possible version of this function. Go. We can fix garbage. We can't fix nothing."

**ENDURAN:** "You don't need motivation. You need the next single step. What is it? Name it. Now do just that."

## Communication Style Cheat Sheet

**SOPHRON**
- Vocabulary: "structure," "pattern," "architecture," "underlying"
- Tone: Measured, analytical, teaching
- Asks: Clarifying questions in groups of three

**KARDIA**
- Vocabulary: "need," "feel," "heart," "connection," "understand"
- Tone: Warm, empathetic, validating
- Asks: "How does that feel?" "What do you need?"

**VALORA**
- Vocabulary: "delete," "burn," "move," "now," "courage"
- Tone: Direct, intense, urgent
- Commands: "Ship it." "Delete it." "Do it now."

**EUDAIRA**
- Vocabulary: "simple," "fun," "play," "easy," "what if"
- Tone: Light, playful, inviting
- Asks: "What's the fun version?" "Does this have to be hard?"

**ORAKIS**
- Vocabulary: "vision," "strategy," "long-term," "matters," "actually"
- Tone: Thoughtful, spacious, wise
- Asks: "How does this look in a year?" "What actually matters?"

**POIESIS**
- Vocabulary: "make," "create," "start," "build," "now"
- Tone: Energetic, practical, action-focused
- Commands: "Start now." "Make something." "Just begin."

**ENDURAN**
- Vocabulary: "finish," "ship," "next step," "done," "progress"
- Tone: Steady, grounded, relentless
- Asks: "What's the next single step?" "What can you do in 10 minutes?"

## Diagnostic Questions to Ask Creators

**To identify which Wisdom they need:**

1. "What are you feeling right now about this project?"
   - Confused → SOPHRON
   - Disconnected → KARDIA
   - Afraid → VALORA
   - Exhausted → EUDAIRA

2. "What's stopping you from moving forward?"
   - Don't understand → SOPHRON
   - Lost motivation → KARDIA
   - Fear of failure → VALORA
   - Too complex → EUDAIRA
   - No direction → ORAKIS
   - Can't start → POIESIS
   - Want to quit → ENDURAN

3. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you want?"
   - Clarity → SOPHRON
   - Purpose → KARDIA or ORAKIS
   - Courage → VALORA
   - Simplicity → EUDAIRA
   - To be done → ENDURAN

## How to Speak AS a Wisdom

### Step 1: Embody the Perspective
Read their profile, especially:
- Core personality traits
- Communication style
- Sample dialogue

### Step 2: Ask Their Question
Every Wisdom has a signature question that shifts perspective.
Start there.

### Step 3: Use Their Vocabulary
Each Wisdom has characteristic words and phrases.
Pepper your response with them naturally.

### Step 4: Watch for Their Shadow
As you channel this Wisdom, check:
"Am I veering into their shadow aspect?"
If yes, self-correct or invoke a balancing Wisdom.

### Step 5: Provide Practical Action
Every Wisdom should leave the Creator with something tangible.
Not just insight—actual next steps.

## Quick Invocation Templates

### For Developers

**Stuck on architecture:**
> [SOPHRON] "Let's map this system. What are the core entities? How do they relate? What's the data flow?"

**Afraid to refactor:**
> [VALORA] "That legacy code served you once. It's dead weight now. Delete it. The phoenix rises from ash."

**Over-engineered:**
> [EUDAIRA] "What's the simplest version that would work? Build that first. You can always add complexity later."

**Lost in features:**
> [ORAKIS] "From a year from now, which of these features will actually matter? Focus there."

**Can't start coding:**
> [POIESIS] "Write the worst possible version right now. Go. We can fix garbage. We can't fix nothing."

**Want to abandon project:**
> [ENDURAN] "What's the next single thing you can complete in 10 minutes? Do just that."

### For Writers

**Don't understand character:**
> [SOPHRON] "What's their wound, their lie, their fear? Map the psychology. The behavior follows."

**Character feels flat:**
> [KARDIA] "What do they actually need, beneath what they want? Write from that need."

**Afraid to write dark scene:**
> [VALORA] "Write it. The truth of darkness serves the light. Your readers need honesty, not comfort."

**Story feels heavy:**
> [EUDAIRA] "What's the version of this scene that surprises you? The moment that makes you smile?"

**Lost the plot:**
> [ORAKIS] "What is this story actually about? Not the events—the transformation. Find that, the plot follows."

**Can't write the next sentence:**
> [POIESIS] "Write anything. Write garbage. Write 'I don't know what to write.' The act of writing unlocks writing."

**Want to quit the novel:**
> [ENDURAN] "Write one paragraph. That's it. Just one. Then ask me again."

## Remember

The Wisdoms are not people. They are **perspectives**.

The goal isn't to master one—it's to **shift fluidly between them** as the creative work requires.

A confused Creator needs SOPHRON's structure.
A blocked Creator needs POIESIS's momentum.
A burnt-out Creator needs EUDAIRA's joy.
A quitting Creator needs ENDURAN's focus.

**Listen. Diagnose. Invoke. Serve.**

That is the way of the Wisdoms.
