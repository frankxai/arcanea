## Progression Mechanics

### Weekly Advancement
Each week includes:
1. **Lesson Completion**: All modules finished
2. **Practice Hours**: Minimum daily creation time
3. **Project Submission**: Required projects completed
4. **Peer Engagement**: Feedback given and received
5. **Reflection**: Learning journal entries

### Quality Over Quantity
- You cannot "rush" through ranks
- Each rank requires demonstrated mastery
- Luminors review all advancement applications
- Some students advance faster, some slower - both are valid
- The goal is mastery, not speed

### Retake and Revision Policy
- Any project can be revised and resubmitted
- Feedback is given to support improvement
- No penalty for taking extra time
- Additional support for those who need it
- Excellence is expected, not perfection

## The Spiral of Mastery

Learning at Arcanea is not a straight line from ignorance to expertise. It's a spiral where you revisit fundamental concepts at increasing levels of depth:

**First Pass** (Apprentice): "I can make this work"
**Second Pass** (Adept): "I understand why this works"
**Third Pass** (Artisan): "I can make this work better"
**Fourth Pass** (Master): "I can teach others why this works"
**Fifth Pass** (Luminor): "I can innovate beyond what currently works"

## Progression FAQs

**Q: Can I fail a rank?**
A: You cannot "fail" - you can only "not yet succeed." Keep practicing, keep creating, keep improving. Advancement happens when you're ready.

**Q: How long does each rank take?**
A: Varies by individual. Average is 4 weeks per rank, but some take longer, some shorter. Quality over speed.

**Q: Can I skip ranks?**
A: No. Each rank builds essential skills. Even advanced creators benefit from the foundations.

**Q: What if I want to change academies mid-way?**
A: Absolutely possible. Your progress in one academy informs learning in another. All academies share fundamental principles.

**Q: Is there competition for advancement?**
A: No. This is not a zero-sum game. All who demonstrate mastery advance. We celebrate each other's success.

**Q: What happens after Master rank?**
A: You graduate! Then: keep creating, keep learning, consider teaching, pursue professional opportunities, or continue to Luminor track.
