# Arcanean Language Guide

> *"Names in Arcanea are not labels—they are resonances. Each name carries the frequency of its bearer."*

**Status**: Canonical Reference
**Purpose**: Naming conventions, phonetics, and language patterns for Arcanea

---

## Core Principles

1. **Names are Resonances** - Names should feel like they vibrate at a frequency
2. **Sound Before Meaning** - How it sounds matters more than what it "means"
3. **No Direct Translation** - Never use obvious Latin/Greek words (Memoria, Praxis, Kairos = AI slop)
4. **Soft Power** - Feminine, flowing energy even in powerful names
5. **Cultural Blend** - Japanese + Fantasy + Mediterranean influences

## Pronunciation Guide

### Stress Patterns

- 2 syllables: stress on FIRST (LEY-la, I-no)
- 3 syllables: stress on SECOND (ly-RIA, a-LE-ra)
- 4 syllables: stress on SECOND (lyss-AN-dria, dra-CO-nia)

### Vowel Sounds

| Written | Pronounced | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| ae | "ay" or "eye" | Aethira = "AY-thir-ah" |
| y | "ee" | Lyria = "LEER-ee-ah" |
| i (end) | "ee" | Amiri = "ah-MEER-ee" |
| a (end) | "ah" | Velora = "veh-LOH-rah" |

## Creating New Names

### Process

1. **Start with the SOUND** - What frequency should this name vibrate at?
2. **Choose a ROOT** - A soft consonant cluster (vel-, lyr-, aer-, thy-)
3. **Add an ENDING** - Match to category (-ia, -ara, -ith, -a)
4. **Say it ALOUD** - Does it flow? Does it feel Arcanean?
5. **Check for SLOP** - Is it just a Latin/Greek word? Reject it.

### Sound Testing

Good Arcanean names should:
- ✓ Flow when spoken aloud
- ✓ Feel like they could be whispered in a sacred space
- ✓ Have a "vibration" quality
- ✓ Work across cultures (not too tied to one language)

Bad names:
- ✗ Sound like corporate branding
- ✗ Are obviously translated words
- ✗ Feel harsh or industrial
- ✗ Could be a startup company name
