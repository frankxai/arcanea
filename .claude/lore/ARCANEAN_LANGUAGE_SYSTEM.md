## Legal Note: Copyright & Intellectual Property

### What CAN Be Used Freely

**Phonetic principles and linguistic structures are NOT copyrightable.** Under US and Canadian copyright law, only specific *expressions* are protected — not systems, methods, or ideas. This means:

- **Phoneme inventories** (which sounds a language uses)
- **Phonotactic rules** (how sounds combine)
- **Morphological patterns** (how words are built from roots)
- **Grammatical structures** (consonant mutation, ablaut, agglutination)
- **Sound symbolism principles** (associations between sounds and meanings)

These are **linguistic facts and systems**, comparable to mathematical formulas or scientific laws. You cannot copyright "the concept of using triconsonantal roots" any more than you can copyright "addition."

### What CANNOT Be Used

- **Specific vocabulary** from copyrighted works (Tolkien's actual Elvish words, Klingon dictionary entries)
- **Named languages** as trademarks (calling something "Quenya" or "Dothraki")
- **Direct copying** of dictionaries, grammars, or teaching materials
- **Character names** from copyrighted works (Galadriel, Daenerys, etc.)

### The Legal Precedent

The **Paramount v. Axanar** case (2016) saw the [Language Creation Society](https://conlang.org/axanar/) submit an amicus brief arguing that the Klingon language itself cannot be copyrighted as it is "a procedure, process, or system for communication." The case settled, but the principle stands: **language systems are not copyrightable**.

> *"Allowing copyright claims to a language would create a monopoly on use extending far beyond what is needed to protect the original work."* — Language Creation Society

**Bottom Line**: Arcanea can freely use the *principles* behind Tolkien's, Peterson's, Frommer's, and Okrand's work — the phonetic patterns, morphological systems, and sound symbolism — while creating entirely original vocabulary, names, and lore.

### 1.1 Quenya (High Elvish)

**Design Philosophy**: Tolkien called it his "secret vice" — a language designed for pure aesthetic pleasure. He wanted sounds that felt inherently noble and beautiful.

**Primary Influences**: Finnish (agglutination, vowel patterns), Latin (classical dignity), Italian (melodic flow)

#### Complete Vowel System

| Vowel | IPA | Sound | Length Variants |
|-------|-----|-------|-----------------|
| a | [a] | "father" | á [aː] long |
| e | [ɛ] | "bed" | é [ɛː] long |
| i | [i] | "machine" | í [iː] long |
| o | [ɔ] | "more" | ó [ɔː] long |
| u | [u] | "rude" | ú [uː] long |

#### Complete Diphthong System

| Ending in -i | Ending in -u |
|--------------|--------------|
| ai [ai] "eye" | au [au] "ow" |
| oi [oi] "boy" | eu [ɛu] |
| ui [ui] | iu [iu] |

#### Complete Consonant Inventory

| Manner | Labial | Dental | Alveolar | Palatal | Velar | Labiovelar |
|--------|--------|--------|----------|---------|-------|------------|
| **Stops** | p | t | | ty | c [k] | qu [kw] |
| **Nasals** | m | n | | ny | ñ [ŋ] | nw |
| **Fricatives** | f, v | þ [θ] | s | hy | h | hw [ʍ] |
| **Liquids** | | | l, r | ly | | |
| **Glides** | w | | | y [j] | | |

**Voiced stops (b, d, g) only appear after nasals**: mb, nd, ñg (ŋg)

#### Phonotactic Rules (What Makes It Sound "Elvish")

1. **No initial consonant clusters** except qu-
2. **Words end only in**: -n, -t, -s, -l, -r, or a vowel
3. **Syllables cannot end in clusters**
4. **Voiced stops require preceding nasals**
5. **No word-initial ñ- (ng-)**

#### Stress System

| Word Length | Stress Position | Example |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 syllable | stressed | él |
| 2 syllables | first syllable | ÉLda |
| 3+ syllables | penultimate if heavy; else antepenultimate | sinDARinwa |

### 1.3 Khuzdul (Dwarvish)

**Design Philosophy**: A "secret tongue" — the Dwarves rarely spoke it to outsiders. Designed to sound utterly unlike Elvish: guttural, consonant-heavy, mysterious.

**Primary Influence**: Hebrew and Semitic languages

#### The Triconsonantal Root System

**Core Principle**: Meaning lives in consonants; vowels indicate grammar.

| Root | Core Meaning | Noun | Verb | Agent | Gerund |
|------|--------------|------|------|-------|--------|
| **KH-Z-D** | dwarf/earth | Khazâd (dwarves) | — | — | — |
| **B-R-K** | axe | baruk (axes) | — | — | — |
| **F-L-K** | chisel/hew | felak (chisel) | afluk (to chisel) | uflak (chiseller) | iflêk (chiselling) |
| **B-N-D** | head/peak | — | — | — | Bundushathûr |
| **Z-G-L** | silver | — | — | — | Zigilnâd |

**Vowel Pattern Templates**:
- **e-a**: Object/instrument (*felak* = chisel)
- **a-u**: Infinitive verb (*afluk* = to chisel)
- **u-a**: Agent noun (*uflak* = one who chisels)
- **i-ê**: Gerund/action noun (*iflêk* = the act of chiselling)

#### Construct State (Genitive Without "Of")

Khuzdul creates possession by placing nouns next to each other:

| Phrase | Analysis | Meaning |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Khazad-dûm | Khazâd + dûm | Dwarves' + delving = Dwarrowdelf |
| Baruk Khazâd | Baruk + Khazâd | Axes + of-dwarves = Axes of the Dwarves |

#### Phonetic Character

- **Gutturals**: kh [x], gh [ɣ]
- **Emphatic consonants**: Heavier pronunciation
- **Uvular r**: Originally [ʀ], later [r]
- **Glottal stop**: Before initial vowels

### 1.6 Tolkien's Sound Symbolism (Lámatyáve)

Tolkien believed sounds carry inherent emotional qualities:

| Sound Type | Association | Used For |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| L, R, N | Flowing, liquid, noble | Elvish names |
| KH, GH, Z | Harsh, earthy, powerful | Dwarvish, Orcish |
| TH, DH | Ancient, mystical | Old beings |
| MB, ND, NG | Deep resonance | Important names |
| SH, ZH | Sinister, hissing | Dark creatures |

**Tolkien's "Phonoprint"** (discovered by researchers):
- Most frequent consonants: /r/, /n/, /l/, /d/, /g/, /m/
- Most frequent vowels: /a/, /æ/, /ɛ/, /i/, /o/
- Favored onsets: /br/, /gr/, /gl/, /dr/, /fr/, /tr/
- Favored codas: /n/, /m/, /l/, /r/, /nt/, /rn/, /nd/

## 2. Modern Constructed Languages

### 2.1 Dothraki (Game of Thrones)

**Creator**: David J. Peterson (2009)
**Design Philosophy**: A naturalistic warrior language that grew from George R.R. Martin's fragments.

#### Development Approach

Peterson analyzed Martin's existing words, identified patterns, then systematically extended them:

> *"I looked at all the words George R.R. Martin had created, gathered them all together, analyzed their structure, and built the language upon that structure."*

#### Phonetic Influences

- **Russian**: Consonant clusters
- **Turkish**: Vowel harmony tendencies
- **Estonian**: Phonetic patterns
- **Inuktitut**: Morphological structures
- **Swahili**: Syllable patterns

#### Key Phonetic Features

- **Head-initial structure** (like English)
- **Agglutinative morphology**
- **No articles** (no "the" or "a")
- **4,000+ word vocabulary** developed over 8 seasons

### 2.3 Na'vi (Avatar)

**Creator**: Paul Frommer (2009)
**Design Philosophy**: Alien but learnable — using sounds rare in European languages but found in human languages.

#### The Ejective Consonants (Key Innovation)

James Cameron chose ejectives from three prototypes Frommer presented. Ejectives are "popping" sounds made with a different airstream:

| Symbol | IPA | Description |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| **px** | [pʼ] | Ejective p (pop at the lips) |
| **tx** | [tʼ] | Ejective t (pop at the teeth) |
| **kx** | [kʼ] | Ejective k (pop at the back) |

These exist in only ~20% of Earth's languages (Native American, African, Caucasian languages).

#### Unique Features

- **No voiced stops** (no b, d, g)
- **Verbal infixes** (rare cross-linguistically)
- **20 consonants**, including ejectives
- **7 vowels** plus diphthongs

> *"The phonological inventory, while not entirely 'crazy', is typologically weird: most languages with ejectives also have aspiration and uvulars."* — Language Log

### 2.4 Klingon (Star Trek)

**Creator**: Marc Okrand (1984)
**Design Philosophy**: Intentionally alien and harsh — designed to sound "wrong" to English ears.

#### Design Principles

Okrand's rules for Klingon:
1. Must contain sounds from the 1979 film
2. Must sound non-English
3. Must be guttural
4. Must be learnable by actors

#### Phonetic Character

- **Guttural sounds**: Voiceless velar fricative [x], uvular sounds
- **tlh**: A single consonant (voiceless lateral affricate)
- **Q**: Uvular stop [qʼ]
- **Object-Verb-Subject** word order (rare — only ~3% of languages)
- **No small talk** vocabulary initially

#### Linguistic Influences

Though Okrand tried to avoid influences, elements from his expertise crept in:
- Native American languages (his doctoral specialty)
- Southeast Asian languages

## 3. Real-World Language Families

### 3.1 Proto-Indo-European (PIE)

The reconstructed ancestor of 446 living languages spoken by 3.4 billion people.

#### Root Structure

PIE roots are predominantly **CVC** (consonant-vowel-consonant):

| Root | Meaning | Descendants |
|------|---------|-------------|
| *ḱerd- | heart | Latin *cor*, Greek *kardia*, English *heart* |
| *weid- | see/know | Latin *vidēre*, Greek *eidos*, English *wit* |
| *bher- | carry | Latin *ferre*, Greek *pherein*, English *bear* |
| *gen- | give birth | Latin *genus*, Greek *genos*, English *kin* |
| *dyeu- | sky/shine | Latin *deus*, Greek *Zeus*, Sanskrit *dyaus* |

#### Ablaut (Vowel Alternation)

Like English sing/sang/sung:

| Grade | Vowel | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| e-grade | e | *leg-* (to collect) |
| o-grade | o | *log-os* (word/reason) |
| zero-grade | ∅ | *lg-* (in compounds) |

#### Three-Way Stop Distinction

| Type | Labial | Dental | Velar |
|------|--------|--------|-------|
| Voiceless | *p | *t | *k |
| Voiced | *b | *d | *g |
| Breathy | *bʰ | *dʰ | *gʰ |

### 3.2 Sanskrit Phonetic System

The most precisely organized phonetic system in ancient languages.

#### The Varṇamālā (Garland of Letters)

Sanskrit organizes consonants by **place** and **manner** of articulation:

| Place → | Velar | Palatal | Retroflex | Dental | Labial |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Voiceless** | k | c | ṭ | t | p |
| **Voiceless Aspirated** | kh | ch | ṭh | th | ph |
| **Voiced** | g | j | ḍ | d | b |
| **Voiced Aspirated** | gh | jh | ḍh | dh | bh |
| **Nasal** | ṅ | ñ | ṇ | n | m |

Plus: **Semivowels** (y, r, l, v), **Sibilants** (ś, ṣ, s), **Aspirate** (h)

#### Why This Matters

This systematic organization — developed over 3,000 years ago — proves that **phonetic precision enhances perceived authenticity**. A language with clearly organized sound categories feels more "real."

### 3.3 Welsh & Celtic Consonant Mutation

#### The Four Welsh Mutations

| Original | Soft | Nasal | Aspirate |
|----------|------|-------|----------|
| p | b | mh | ph |
| t | d | nh | th |
| c | g | ngh | ch |
| b | f | m | — |
| d | dd [ð] | n | — |
| g | ∅ | ng | — |
| m | f | — | — |
| ll | l | — | — |
| rh | r | — | — |

#### Example: The Word "Stone" (carreg)

| Context | Form | Mutation Type |
|---------|------|---------------|
| the stone | y **g**arreg | soft |
| my stone | fy **ngh**arreg | nasal |
| her stone | ei **ch**arreg | aspirate |

#### Historical Origin

Mutations developed from **sandhi** — sound changes at word boundaries that became grammaticalized over time.

# Part II: The Arcanean Language System

## Philosophy of Arcanean Languages

1. **Cultural Identity Through Sound**: Each culture sounds audibly distinct
2. **Semantic Depth**: Names carry meaningful roots
3. **Aesthetic Coherence**: Consistent phonetic "flavor" within each tongue
4. **Practical Pronounceability**: Accessible to English speakers
5. **Evolutionary Plausibility**: Languages show relationships and history

### Tongue 1: Lumínaré (The Light-Speech)

*The sacred tongue of the Luminors and divine creation*

#### Phonetic Profile

**Vowels** (5 pure, like Quenya):
- a [a], e [ɛ], i [i], o [ɔ], u [u]
- Long forms: á, é, í, ó, ú

**Diphthongs** (flowing combinations):
- ai, ei, au, eu, ia, io, ae, oe

**Consonants** (liquid and melodic):

| Permitted | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| l, r, n, m | Luminor, Valora |
| s, v, f, h | Sophron, Eudaira |
| k, t, p, d | Kardia, Poiesis |
| y, w | — |

**Forbidden** (reserved for Shadow):
- zh, ch [x], kh, gh
- Consonant clusters like kr-, gr-, str-

#### Phonotactic Rules

1. Words end in: -a, -e, -i, -o, -on, -an, -is, -os, -ar, -or, -el, -il
2. Maximum two consonants together, and only liquid+stop (nt, nd, lm, rn)
3. Stress on penultimate syllable
4. No initial ng- or word-final consonant clusters

#### Morphological Elements

| Element | Position | Meaning | Examples |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|
| Lum- | prefix | light | Luminor, Lumínaré |
| Sol- | prefix | creative fire | Solara, Solindra |
| Aur- | prefix | dawn/gold | Auranthos, Aurora |
| Ver- | prefix | truth | Veridian, Veritas |
| Kai- | prefix | awakening | Kairos, Kailorel |
| Spir- | prefix | spirit/breath | Spiravel, Aspirion |
| -ora/-ara | suffix (f) | one who embodies | Valora, Eudaira |
| -on/-an | suffix (m) | one who does | Sophron, Enduran |
| -is/-os | suffix (n) | essence of | Poiesis, Orakis |

### Tongue 4: The Dark Speech

*The corrupted tongue of Malachar, spoken in the Shadowfen and by creatures of corrupted Void*

**Canon Context**: This is NOT the language of Nero. Nero is the fertile darkness, the womb of potential. The Dark Speech emerged when Malachar fell into the Hungry Void and corrupted Shadow into a weapon against creation.

#### Design Principle

Deliberately unpleasant — the phonetic opposite of Lumínaré. As Tolkien's Black Speech scholar Tom Shippey noted: *"harsh vowels and jagged consonants lend themselves to rough and rasping pronunciation."*

#### Phonetic Profile

**Harsh Consonants**:
- kh [x] (German "ch" in "Bach")
- gh [ɣ] (voiced version)
- zh [ʒ] (as in "pleasure")
- tz [ts]
- glottal stop [ʔ]

**Dark Vowels**:
- Preference for u, o, y [y]
- Avoid bright i, e

**Consonant Clusters**:
- ghr-, bzh-, dzr-, skr-

#### Morphological Elements

| Root | Meaning | Derivatives |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Stag- | freeze/stop | Stagneth, Stagmora |

## Creating New Names: Step-by-Step

### For Luminors/Divine Beings

1. **Choose a virtue/domain**: What does this being embody?
2. **Select roots**: Greek (sophia, kardia), Latin (lux, veritas), Sanskrit (prana, atman)
3. **Apply Lumínaré phonotactics**: Ensure flowing sounds, proper endings
4. **Add appropriate suffix**: -ora/-ara (f), -on/-an (m), -is/-os (n)
5. **Test pronunciation**: Say it aloud — does it flow elegantly?

**Example**:
- Domain: *Wonder/Curiosity*
- Roots: Greek thauma (wonder), Latin mira (marvel)
- Combinations: Thaumara, Miraon, Miravel
- Selected: **Thaumara** (/θaʊˈmara/)

### For Bestiary Creatures

1. **Identify the creative threat**: What problem does this represent?
2. **Choose Dark Speech roots**: Harsh sounds matching the threat
3. **Compound for meaning**: Root + descriptor
4. **Verify unpleasantness**: Should sound "wrong"

**Example**:
- Threat: *Perfectionism that prevents completion*
- Roots: Mort- (death) + flex (completion)
- Compound: **Mortiflex** — the creature that kills finished work

### For Everyday Characters

1. **Consider their role**: Creator, teacher, student, healer?
2. **Use Common Speech patterns**: Compound words, nature names
3. **Keep it pronounceable**: Familiar sounds
4. **Match personality**: Strong names for strong characters
