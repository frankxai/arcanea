## Why this arc, why now

Arc 1 (*Seven Impossible People*) is the franchise's **entry point** and its most buildable unit: a single
contained incident — a Void Ascendant strike on an Underground haven in the Solar Sector — that forces seven
strangers from seven origin classes into one fight and ends by founding the first Starbound Crew.

It is the right thing to *progress the world* through because the incident **changes the institution
permanently**: the Corps Charter is amended to recognize cross-origin crews. That is a real world-state delta,
not set dressing. Season One turns the synopsis into eight episodes and stages the canonical consequences.

**Progression discipline (Musk lens):** every episode below must move the world from State A → State B with a
traceable cause. No episode is allowed to leave the world where it found it.

## The world before (State A) — canon baseline

- The Starlight Corps exists as an **Arcan-dominated institution**; cross-origin crews are informal at best.
- The Gate-Touched Underground runs a secret haven network; havens in the Solar Sector are hidden, fragile.
- The Void Ascendants are a recruiting insurgency arguing *the system only serves its own* — and they are not wrong.
- The seven who will become the Flagship Team have never met. Each is mid-life in their own origin class.
- The seal on the Shadowfen holds; belief in the Gate system's legitimacy is eroding but intact.

## The Season One episode map (8 episodes)

Each episode is a buildable unit: a logline, the world-state movement, the moral question, and the canon hooks.
The Operator writes each as a prose episode (≈2,000–3,500 words) in `.arcanea/lore/staging/season-one/`.

### E01 — "Wrong Place, Wrong Hour"
**Logline.** Seven people are pulled toward the same ruined street in the Solar Sector by seven unrelated reasons.
**Moves.** Establishes each protagonist in their origin class, alone, competent, unaware of the others.
**Question.** Who are you before you're a team? · **Hooks.** FLAGSHIP_TEAM_V2.md (all seven); Solar Sector geography.

### E02 — "The Cascade"
**Logline.** Ryn goes mid-cascade against three Ascendant operatives; the night's fight detonates.
**Moves.** The strike lands; the haven is under threat; the seven collide for the first time.
**Question.** Is raw power a gift or a liability when no one trained you? · **Hooks.** Gate-Touched cascade mechanics.

### E03 — "The Architect's Reason"
**Logline.** Cut to Selvyn Ashvale, who designed this strike — and *why*. Not a villain monologue; an argument.
**Moves.** Establishes D6 and seeds D4. The audience understands the enemy is partly right.
**Question.** What do you do when the enemy diagnoses a real disease? · **Hooks.** VOID_ASCENDANTS.md (Selvyn).

### E04 — "Seven Disagreements"
**Logline.** The seven survive the first hour by fighting *each other* almost as much as the Ascendants.
**Moves.** Ryn distrusts Kaelindra's instinct to contain; Taura finds Caelum's passivity infuriating; Solenne's
chosen name is used wrong, twice, and corrected. No trust yet — only proximity.
**Question.** Can people who disagree about everything still choose each other? · **Hooks.** FLAGSHIP_TEAM_V2 dynamics.

### E05 — "What Mireth Knew"
**Logline.** Mireth was inside the situation before it began, on a timeline that predates the crisis.
**Moves.** Introduces the franchise's deep mystery thread (pays off in Arc 4) without resolving it. Restraint.
**Question.** How much foreknowledge is help, and how much is a different kind of violation? · **Hooks.** Mireth profile.

### E06 — "The Haven Holds"
**Logline.** Solenne anchors an Ironhold Stance to keep a transit structure from burying two families while the
others break the strike. The haven is exposed to the city — and survives.
**Moves.** Stages **D5**. First proof that visibility need not mean death for the Underground.
**Question.** Is being seen the same as being safe? · **Hooks.** STARLIGHT_CORPS_CODEX (Ironhold Stance); Underground.

### E07 — "The Debrief in the Ruin"
**Logline.** Kaelindra runs the first crew briefing in history in a bombed-out corridor — before anyone calls it that.
**Moves.** The seven recognize what they did: seven origins made real-time decisions no single-origin team could.
Seeds **D4** — the Ascendants will weaponize exactly this discovery.
**Question.** Is choosing each other the same thing as trusting each other? · **Hooks.** STORY_ENGINE Arc 1 debrief.

### E08 — "Amended" *(season finale)*
**Logline.** Seravyn Duskmere authorizes the team *after the fact*; the Charter is amended; the Reaches object.
**Moves.** Stages **D1, D2, D3**. The institution is permanently changed. The cost — Cassiel's dissent — is real.
**Question.** When the system finally opens, who decides it was allowed to? · **Hooks.** Crew Solara; Archon dissent.

## Continuity guardrails (K-Pop lens — zero ragged edges)

- **Names are exact.** Kaelindra Voss, Ryn, Velindris, Solenne Ashmark, Taura Skein + Vaerith, Caelum, Mireth.
  Selvyn Ashvale (Herald of Fracture). Seravyn Duskmere (Crew Solara Archon). Archon Cassiel (dissenter).
- **Frequencies are exact** (CANON_LOCKED Ten Gates table). Never invent a Gate or reassign a frequency.
- **Nero ≠ evil.** Shadow = corrupted Void. Never collapse the duality.
- **Solenne's chosen name** is a continuity device, not a typo — track it deliberately across E04/E08.
- **Mireth's foreknowledge** is seeded, never explained, in Season One. Resolution belongs to Arc 4.

## What "meaningful" means here (the test)

A reader who finishes E08 must be able to answer: *what is true about Arcanea now that wasn't true at E01?*
The answer is **D1–D6**. If a draft episode can be cut without changing that answer, it failed the progression
test and the Queen rejects it.
